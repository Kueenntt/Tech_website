The Game

Basketball is a fast-paced, high-energy team sport played on a rectangular court. Two teams, each with five players on the floor, aim to outscore each other by shooting a round ball through a hoop that stands 10 feet off the ground. The hoop is mounted on a backboard, and each successful shot scores points depending on how far the shooter was when they released the ball.

The game starts with a “jump ball,” where two opposing players jump to tap the ball to their teammates. Throughout the match, players dribble (bounce) the ball while moving, pass it to teammates, or take shots. Defenders try to block shots and steal the ball, relying on strategy, speed, and teamwork.

Players

In basketball, each player has a unique role that contributes to the team's success. There are guards, forwards, and centers. Guards are typically quicker and handle the ball more; forwards combine speed and strength; centers are often the tallest and protect the rim. Every player trains hard — working on their dribbling, shooting, passing, and defensive abilities.

Players also rely on teamwork and communication. A great play often comes from sharing the ball and predicting where teammates will be. Defense is just as important — players must be able to guard their opponents, contest shots, and secure rebounds.
